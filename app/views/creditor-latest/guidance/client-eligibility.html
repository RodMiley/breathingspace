Applying for a breathing space moratorium

Debtors can only enter the Debt Respite Scheme using a debt adviser.

Anyone who cannot or is unlikely to be able to repay their debts can apply to a debt adviser for a Breathing Space.

Although all applications for the scheme must be considered, the debt adviser might decide a Breathing Space is not appropriate for a debtor.

For example, if a person could pay their debts with some budgeting help, or they already have assets that could be sold to clear the debt, a Breathing Space would not be the right solution. A Breathing Space might also not be appropriate for a someone who can enter a more suitable debt solution straight away, without needing the scheme’s protections.

Debtor eligibility for a breathing space moratorium

Before a debt adviser can put someone into Breathing Space, they must confirm the debtor meets all the conditions for a moratorium. These are that the debtor must:

  • A debtor must owe a qualifying debt to a creditor
  • A debtor must live or usually resides in England or Wales
  • A debtor must not have a debt relief order (DRO), an individual voluntary arrangement (IVA), an interim order, or be an undischarged bankrupt at the time they apply
  • A debtor must not already be in Breathing Space or have been one in the last 12 months at the time they apply

The debtor must also meet both of the conditions. These are that:

  • A debtor cannot, or is unlikely to be able to, repay all or some of their debt
  • The debt adviser thinks that a moratorium is appropriate for the debtor

Debtor eligibility for a mental health crisis moratorium

The debtor must still meet the same criteria and conditions for a breathing space moratorium, but they must also be receiving mental health crisis treatment at the time they apply.

  • There is no limit to how many times a debtor can enter a mental health crisis moratorium.
