Managing statutory debt repayment plans (SDRP) Guidance for creditors and debt advisers managing SDRPs

This page provides guidance from the Insolvency Service for creditors and debt advisers

Contents

  • – Summary
  • – Debtor eligibility
  • – Objecting to a plan
  • – Revoking a plan
  • – SFS income and expenditure tool

Summary

This guidance is for you if:

  • you are a creditor
  • you are a money or debt adviser

Debtor eligibility

For a person to be eligible to enter an SDRP, they must:

  • owe a ‘qualifying’ debt to a creditor
  • live in England and Wales or an ordinarily resident in England or Wales
  • not be subject to a debt relief order
  • not be subject to an interim order or IVA (individual voluntary arrangement)
  • not be an undischarged bankrupt
  • not be subject to another plan in relation to a joint debtor
  • not be subject to another plan which was revoked or completed within 12 months of the date of the application

Objecting to a plan

There are regulations restricting creditor objections to a proposed plan.

If you want to object to a proposed plan you must do it within a 14-day statutory period.

The grounds for objecting include:

  • the plan is not in the creditor’s interest
  • debts are missing from a plan
  • a proposed payment schedule
  • debtor eligibility
  • the accuracy of a plan
  • income and expenditure disputes

Revoking a plan

Debt advice providers (DAPs) can revoke, or cancel, a plan under certain circumstances.

There are 2 types of revocation:

  • mandatory
  • discretionary

If a revocation has been submitted by a DAP and registered, creditors will receive notification from the Secretary of State.

After a plan has been revoked, creditors can restart enforcement action to recover a debt.

SFS income and expenditure tool

The Standard Financial Statement (SFS) is a universal income and expenditure statement, with a single set of spending guidelines.

It offers a tool to summarise a debtor’s income and outgoings, along with their debts.

It is used mainly for people seeking debt advice.

It is also used by debt advice providers and other relevant organisations to assess what a person can repay.
